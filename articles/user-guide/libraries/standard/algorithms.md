### <a name="quantum-phase-estimation"></a><span data-ttu-id="fbd52-218">Estimación de fase cuántica</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-218">Quantum Phase Estimation</span></span> ###

<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-219">Una aplicación especialmente importante de la transformación de Fourier de Quantum es obtener información sobre los vectores propios de los operadores unitarios, un problema conocido como *estimación de fase*.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-219">One particularly important application of the quantum Fourier transform is to learn the eigenvalues of unitary operators, a problem known as *phase estimation*.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-220">Considere la posibilidad de una $U unitario $ y un estado $ \ket{\phi} $, de modo que $ \ket{\phi} $ sea un eigenstate de $U $ con Unknown eigenvalue $ \phi $, \begin{Equation} U\ket {\ Phi} = \phi\ket{\phi}.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-220">Consider a unitary $U$ and a state $\ket{\phi}$ such that $\ket{\phi}$ is an eigenstate of $U$ with unknown eigenvalue $\phi$, \begin{equation} U\ket{\phi} = \phi\ket{\phi}.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-221">\end{Equation} si solo tenemos acceso a $U $ como Oracle, podremos obtener información sobre la fase $ \phi $ mediante el uso de que $Z $ rotaciones aplicadas al destino de una operación controlada se propagan de nuevo al control.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-221">\end{equation} If we only have access to $U$ as an oracle, then we can learn the phase $\phi$ by utilizing that $Z$ rotations applied to the target of a controlled operation propagate back onto the control.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-222">Supongamos que $V $ es una aplicación controlada de $U $, de modo que \begin{align} V (\ket {0} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = \ket {0} \otimes \ket{\phi} \\ \\ \textrm{and} V (\ket {1} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = e ^ {i \phi} \ket {1} \otimes \ket{\phi}.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-222">Suppose that $V$ is a controlled application of $U$, such that \begin{align} V (\ket{0} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & =            \ket{0} \otimes \ket{\phi} \\\\ \textrm{ and } V (\ket{1} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = e^{i \phi} \ket{1} \otimes \ket{\phi}.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-223">\end{align}, por linearity, \begin{align} V (\ket{+} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = \frac{(\ket {0} \otimes \ket{\phi}) + e ^ {i \phi} (\ket {1} \otimes \ket{\phi})} {\sqrt {2} }.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-223">\end{align} Then, by linearity, \begin{align} V(\ket{+} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = \frac{ (\ket{0} \otimes \ket{\phi}) + e^{i \phi} (\ket{1} \otimes \ket{\phi}) }{\sqrt{2}}.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-224">\end{align} podemos recopilar términos para encontrar que \begin{align} V (\ket{+} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = \frac{\ket {0} + e ^ {i \phi} \ket {1} } {\sqrt {2} } \otimes \ket{\phi} \\ \\ & = (R_1 (\phi) \ket{+}) \otimes \ket{\phi}, \end{align} donde $R _ _ 1 es la unitario aplicada por la <xref:microsoft.quantum.intrinsic.r1> operación.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-224">\end{align} We can collect terms to find that \begin{align} V(\ket{+} \otimes \ket{\phi}) & = \frac{\ket{0} + e^{i \phi} \ket{1}}{\sqrt{2}} \otimes \ket{\phi} \\\\ & = (R_1(\phi) \ket{+}) \otimes \ket{\phi}, \end{align} where $R_1$ is the unitary applied by the <xref:microsoft.quantum.intrinsic.r1> operation.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-225">Y se colocan de forma diferente, el efecto de aplicar $V $ es precisamente el mismo que aplicar $R _ 1 _ 1 con un ángulo desconocido, aunque solo se tiene acceso a $V $ como Oracle.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-225">Put differently, the effect of applying $V$ is precisely the same as applying $R_1$ with an unknown angle, even though we only have access to $V$ as an oracle.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-226">Por lo tanto, en el resto de este debate se tratará la estimación de fases en términos de $R _ 1 (\phi) $, que se implementan mediante el uso de la *fase Kickback*.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-226">Thus, for the rest of this discussion we will discuss phase estimation in terms of $R_1(\phi)$, which we implement by using so-called *phase kickback*.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-227">Dado que el registro de destino y el control permanecen inalterados después de este proceso, podemos volver a usar $ \ket{\phi} $ como destino de una aplicación controlada de $U ^ $2 para preparar un segundo control qubit en el estado $R _ 1 (2 \phi) \ket{+} $.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-227">Since the control and target register remain untangled after this process, we can reuse $\ket{\phi}$ as the target of a controlled application of $U^2$ to prepare a second control qubit in the state $R_1(2 \phi) \ket{+}$.</span></span>
<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-228">Continuando de este modo, podemos obtener un registro con el formato \begin{align} \ket{\psi} & = \ sum_ {j = 0} ^ n R_1 (2 ^ j \phi) \ket{+} \\ \\ & \propto \ bigotimes_ {j = 0} ^ {n} \left (\ket {0} + \exp (i 2 ^ {j} \phi) \ket {1} \right) \\ \\ & \propto \ sum_ {k = 0} ^ {2 ^ n-1} \exp (i \phi k) \ket{k} \end{align} donde $n $ es el número de bits de precisión que se requiere. y donde hemos usado $ {} \propto {} $ para indicar que hemos suprimido el factor de normalización de $1/\sqrt{2 ^ n} $.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-228">Continuing in this way, we can obtain a register of the form \begin{align} \ket{\psi} & = \sum_{j = 0}^n R_1(2^j \phi) \ket{+} \\\\ & \propto \bigotimes_{j=0}^{n} \left(\ket{0} + \exp(i 2^{j} \phi) \ket{1}\right) \\\\ & \propto \sum_{k = 0}^{2^n - 1} \exp(i \phi k) \ket{k} \end{align} where $n$ is the number of bits of precision that we require, and where we have used ${} \propto {}$ to indicate that we have suppressed the normalization factor of $1 / \sqrt{2^n}$.</span></span>

<span data-ttu-id="fbd52-229">Si damos por hecho que $ \phi = 2 \pi p/2 ^ k $ para un entero $p $, se reconocerá como $ \ket{\psi} = \operatorname{QFT} \ket{p_0 p_1 \dots p_n} $, donde $p _j $ es el $j ^ {\textrm{TH}} $ bit de $2 \pi \phi $.</span><span class="sxs-lookup"><span data-stu-id="fbd52-229">If we assume that $\phi = 2 \pi p / 2^k$ for an integer $p$, then we recognize this as $\ket{\psi} = \operatorname{QFT} \ket{p_0 p_1 \dots p_n}$, where $p_j$ is the $j^{\textrm{th}}$ bit of $2 \pi \phi$.</span></span>
